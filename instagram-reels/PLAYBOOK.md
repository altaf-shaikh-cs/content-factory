# Instagram Reels PLAYBOOK — the craft baseline

This channel runs without an analytics export, so this file is its feedback loop: the known rules of the Reels game. **The Scout, Strategist, Scriptwriters, and Editor all read it every run.** Keep it sharp as you learn what works. No numbers are invented here — these are platform mechanics and craft heuristics, not performance claims.

---

## §1 — The only metric that matters: retention

Reels reach is driven by **watch-time and completion rate**, amplified by **shares and saves**. Likes barely matter. Everything below serves one goal: keep the viewer watching, and give them a reason to send/save it.

- **Hook the first 1-2 seconds or lose them.** Most scrollers leave in under 3s. The opening frame + first line decide everything.
- **Front-load the payoff tease.** Tell them what they'll get, then make them watch to get it.
- **Design for the loop.** A reel that ends where it began (or sets up a re-watch) racks up watch-time. End on a line that makes the first frame land harder.
- **Shorter wins for reach.** 7-15s reels complete more often → more reach. Go longer (20-40s talking-head, up to ~90s tutorial) only when the value needs it.

---

## §2 — The hook (0-2s) — three layers at once

A reel hook is **visual + text + spoken**, all firing immediately:

1. **Visual:** motion, a surprising frame, a screen mid-action — never a static title card or a face just sitting there.
2. **On-screen text:** a bold, short line readable in <1s. This is what silent scrollers read first (most watch muted).
3. **Spoken/VO line:** the same promise in voice, no warm-up.

Hook patterns that work:
- **Contrarian:** "Everyone's wrong about AI coding tools."
- **Result-first:** "This built a working app while I made coffee."
- **Curiosity gap:** "I tested both so you don't have to. One lost badly."
- **Stakes/mistake:** "Stop building agents like this."
- **Numbered promise:** "3 ways your AI workflow is leaking time."

Banned openings: "Hey guys", "In this video", a logo sting, a slow zoom on a face, "Don't forget to like and subscribe" up front.

---

## §3 — Structure: timed beats, not paragraphs

Every script is a sequence of beats. Each beat = `[timestamp] · On-screen text · Spoken/VO · Visual`. Rules:

- **One idea per beat.** Cut on the idea change — fast cuts hold attention.
- **Open a loop, close it late.** Tease the answer in the hook, pay it off near the end.
- **No dead air.** If a beat doesn't add tension or value, delete it.
- **Captions/overlays always on** — assume muted viewing. The reel must make sense with sound off.

Typical arcs:
- **Demo:** hook (the result) → "here's how" → 2-3 quick steps → the payoff again → CTA.
- **Hot-take:** hook (the claim) → why everyone believes the opposite → your evidence → the reframe → CTA.
- **Story:** hook (the moment) → what you tried → the surprise → the lesson → CTA.
- **Listicle:** hook (the promise + count) → item 1 → item 2 → … → strongest item last → CTA.

## §4 — Faceless vs on-camera

| | FACELESS | ON-CAMERA |
|---|----------|-----------|
| Best for | demos, screen-recordings, listicles, evergreen tutorials | opinions, stories, trust-building, build-in-public |
| Script is | on-screen text + VO + screen/B-roll cues | spoken lines `[to camera]` + B-roll overlays |
| Pro | scalable, no setup, batch-able | higher trust, more shareable opinions, builds a face for the brand |
| Watch for | can feel generic — VO energy + tight cuts fix it | needs energy in the first 2s; no slow settling-in |

The Strategist picks per reel. Demos almost always faceless; hot-takes and stories often win on-camera.

---

## §5 — Audio

- **Trending audio = reach.** Best for faceless/text reels where the audio carries vibe. Note: name the *kind* of trending sound to search for (the actual trending track changes daily).
- **Original voiceover = clarity + evergreen.** Best when the words are the value (tutorials, takes). Won't ride a trend wave but won't expire either.
- A reel using trending audio should still make sense muted (text overlays carry it).

---

## §6 — Caption & hashtags

- **First line is a second hook** — it's what shows above "...more". Don't waste it restating the title; add a reason to expand.
- Keep the body short; end with a **CTA** (comment prompt, save reason, follow reason).
- **3-5 relevant hashtags.** Mix niche (#aiengineering, #buildinpublic) over broad (#ai, #tech). Broad tags drown you; niche tags reach the right people. Never spam 15+.
- No emojis (project rule) unless Altaf asks.

---

## §7 — CTA: earn the follow/save/comment

- **Save-bait:** "Save this before you build your next agent." (works for demos/listicles)
- **Comment-bait:** a genuine question or a "which side are you on" for hot-takes.
- **Follow-bait:** "Part 2 tomorrow" / "Following along? I post one of these a day."
- **Public-shareable only.** Never "DM me to try the internal tool." Strip proprietary access offers.

---

## §8 — Reach suppressors (avoid)

- Slow intro before the hook.
- A static title card as frame 1.
- Reposted TikTok with a visible watermark (IG suppresses it).
- Spammy hashtag walls.
- No on-screen text (loses the muted majority).
- Overlong with no payoff escalation.
- Manufactured outrage / engagement bait that doesn't match the content.

---

## §9 — Rookie-mistake checklist (the Editor scores against this)

- [ ] Hook lands in the **first 1-2 seconds** (visual + text + spoken).
- [ ] Readable with **sound off** (on-screen text carries the meaning).
- [ ] **One clear payoff**, teased early and delivered.
- [ ] **No dead beats** — every segment earns the next.
- [ ] Length matches the goal (short for reach, longer only if value needs it).
- [ ] **CTA is public-shareable** and gives a real reason to act.
- [ ] Caption first line is a second hook; **3-5 niche hashtags**.
- [ ] **Audio approach** named.
- [ ] No company/internal-tool names; no invented stats; no emojis (unless asked).
- [ ] Style (faceless/on-camera) fits the content.

---

## §10 — Reasons people follow after one reel (the follow-triggers)

Aim a reel at one of these:
- **TIL** — taught me something useful fast.
- **SAVE** — I'll need this later (tutorial/checklist).
- **SAME** — relatable; it's my experience too (story).
- **DEBATE** — I have an opinion on this take (hot-take).
- **WOW** — that result is impressive (demo).

Name the target follow-trigger in the plan; it keeps the script pointed.

A few years ago, learning to code was enough.

Today, coding is the easy part.

The difficult part is knowing:

- What to build
- How to break down a problem
- How to debug when AI gives a wrong answer
- How to design systems that actually scale
- How to ask the right questions

I see a lot of developers spending hours collecting courses, tutorials, prompts, frameworks, and AI tools.

But their fundamentals are weak.

And that's becoming more dangerous in the AI era.

Because AI amplifies your strengths.

It also amplifies your weaknesses.

A developer with strong fundamentals can become 10x faster with AI.

A developer with weak fundamentals can become 10x confused.

That's why whenever someone asks me:

"Should I focus on DSA?"
"Should I learn System Design?"
"Should I learn AI?"
"Should I learn React?"

My answer is usually the same.

Don't chase tools.

Build strong foundations.

Tools will change.

Frameworks will change.

AI models will change.

The ability to think clearly and solve problems won't.

That's the skill that compounds for decades.

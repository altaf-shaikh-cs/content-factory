## Core Insight
"Say the word" is Claude's signature offer — it signals that Claude has already done the thinking and is waiting for one instruction to execute. Most people walk right past it.

## Target Audience
Engineers and developers who use Claude Code or Claude daily — people who have seen this phrase appear in their sessions and never stopped to notice what it actually means.

## Structure
1. The phrase "say the word" appears constantly at the end of Claude's responses
2. In real life, "say the word" is what someone says when the preparation is already done — the commitment is made, they're standing by
3. Claude uses it the same way: it's analyzed, planned, and is sitting at the starting line waiting for your go-ahead
4. Most people don't give it — they ask another question, start a fresh chat, go back to doing it themselves

## CTA
What's the last thing Claude offered to do for you, that you never followed through on?

## Tone Range
Conversational, witty, a touch of self-aware humor — the post should feel like a sharp observation, not a lecture

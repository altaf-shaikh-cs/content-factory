## Final Post

You don't need better prompts.

You need to stop ignoring the ones Claude already gave you.

Every serious Claude session ends the same way. It lays out the plan, maps the steps, and then — right at the bottom — it writes: "Say the word and I'll get started."

I walked past it every time. Already typing the next question.

"Say the word" has a specific meaning outside AI. It's what someone says when they're already committed, already prepared, and just waiting for your go-ahead. The hard part is done. The only thing left is you.

Claude uses it the same way.

It's not jargon. It's not a quirk of the model. It's Claude telling you: the plan is ready, the context is loaded, I've done the thinking. Your one job is to say yes.

The best prompt you can give Claude is the one it already asked for.

The developers getting the most out of Claude aren't the ones asking smarter questions. They're the ones who, when Claude offers to execute, actually let it.

Say the word.

What's the last thing Claude offered to do for you, that you never followed through on?

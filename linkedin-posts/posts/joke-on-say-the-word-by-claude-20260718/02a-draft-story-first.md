I keep a bad habit when using Claude.

I ask it to analyze something. It lays out a plan. And then, right at the end, it says: "Say the word and I'll get started."

I type my next question.

That phrase has appeared in my sessions more times than I can count. I walked past it every single time.

It took me a while to notice what it actually means.

In real life, "say the word" is what someone says when the preparation is already done. The commitment is made. They're standing by. They just need your go-ahead.

That's exactly what Claude is doing.

It's not asking whether you want help. It's already done the analysis, laid out the approach, and is sitting at the starting line. The only thing it needs is your permission to run.

Most of us don't give it.

We ask another question. Start a new chat. Go back to doing it ourselves.

The most underused prompt in AI isn't some clever system instruction.

It's: okay, go ahead.

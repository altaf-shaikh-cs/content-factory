One tool opened a browser, navigated the full flow, and confirmed the implementation. On its own.

The other needed three more prompts to get close.

Same task. Same codebase. Very different experience.

I've been building a personal fitness web app — frontend work, nothing exotic.

I brought in two AI tools: Claude and Codex.

Here's what actually happened.

Claude generates clean code. That part isn't in question.

But getting it to understand the full intention — and act on it without hand-holding — that's a different game.

I kept re-explaining. It kept asking for more context. Three more prompts to get close to what I meant on the first ask.

Codex was different.

It understood the intention on the first pass.

Made the changes. Spun up the browser. Navigated the full flow itself.

Then confirmed whether the implementation was correct.

No back and forth. No re-explaining.

Just: done.

## Core Insight
When one AI tool autonomously acts on your intention (opens browser, tests, confirms)
and another requires three re-prompts to get there, that gap is the real benchmark.

## Target Audience
Developers and technical builders who are actively using AI coding tools —
specifically people who use more than one tool and are developing opinions about
which fits which job.

## Structure
1. Hook — the concrete contrast: one tool acted, the other needed more from me
2. Context — what I was building and why both tools were in play
3. What actually happened — specific, observed behavior (no editorializing)
4. The real lesson — this is a tool-fit insight, not a hot take on either product
5. CTA — open question to the audience

## CTA
Which AI tool are you actually using for frontend development — and how much
re-prompting does it take to get what you want?

## Tone Range
conversational · builder-honest · observational (not hypey, not academic)

## Hashtags

## Image Brief
Numbers/data points to carry:
  · "3 prompts" (what one tool needed)
  · "1 pass" (what the other needed)
  · "Experiment #1" (series framing)
  · "Frontend dev" (context label)
Tagline: "Right tool. Right job."
Framing: side-by-side contrast

## Angle Assignments
N=2 — using the two fixed angles:
  - Angle A: Story-first — open with the moment of contrast (watching one tool
    spin up a browser while I was still re-explaining to the other), let the
    insight about tool-fit emerge through the narrative
  - Angle B: Bold claim-first — lead with the provocative conclusion
    (for frontend dev, one tool is meaningfully ahead right now),
    then back it with the specific observed evidence

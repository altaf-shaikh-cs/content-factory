Most Gen AI learning content is teaching you things that will change next month.

Papers, tools, frameworks, APIs — the specifics shift constantly.

What doesn't shift is the foundation.

There's a small set of core concepts that stay stable — and if you understand those, the moving parts start making sense. If you skip them, every new thing just adds to the noise.

I built a Level 1 roadmap around this idea.

Not a "complete guide to Gen AI." I kept it deliberately short.

Only the fundamentals that matter. Only the things that hold across whatever comes next.

Two uses for it:

1. You're getting started — it gives you a clear path instead of a pile of tabs
2. You're already in it — it tells you which fundamentals you might have skipped

It's not exhaustive. That's not the goal.

A Level 2 is coming. But Level 1 first.

Check where you are on this: https://roadmap.sh/r/getting-started-with-gen-ai-level-1

Feedback welcome — I'll build the advanced version next.

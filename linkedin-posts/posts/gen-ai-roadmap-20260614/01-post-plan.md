# Post Plan — gen-ai-roadmap

## Core Insight
Most people trying to get into Gen AI drown in content that changes every week — but the fundamentals that actually matter are short, stable, and learnable in a single focused path.

## Target Audience
Entry-to-mid career software engineers in India (primarily Mumbai, Bengaluru, Delhi) who want to start with Gen AI but feel overwhelmed by the volume and pace of new information.

## Structure
1. The problem: Gen AI content is everywhere, it changes daily, and it's hard to know where to start or whether you're on the right track
2. The reframe: There's a small, stable core — fundamentals that don't shift week to week
3. What Altaf built: A minimal Level 1 roadmap that covers only the essentials — deliberately kept short to avoid overload
4. How to use it: Check where you stand, find your gap, fill it — then the advanced stuff makes more sense
5. CTA: Link to the roadmap + invite feedback

## CTA
Drop the link and invite the audience to check where they land: "Check where you are on this: [link]. Feedback welcome — I'll build the advanced version next."

## Tone Range
Conversational, builder-honest, slightly provocative (the curated-not-exhaustive framing challenges the "cover everything" instinct)

## Hashtags
#GenAI #MachineLearning #AIEngineering #SoftwareEngineering #LearnAI

## Image Brief
Stats/contrast pairs:
  - Level 1 only (not trying to cover everything)
  - Core fundamentals: stable, not changing daily
  - One clear path vs. infinite noise
Tagline: "Start here. Not everywhere."
Framing: Before (overwhelm, infinite content) / After (one clear path, stable fundamentals)

## Performance Context
- Best performing angle so far: Bold claim-first — claude-vs-codex (5.09% eng. rate, above 3.77% baseline)
- Hook styles that drove engagement: Comparison framing (A vs B) — audiences have a stake in the outcome
- Hook styles that drove passive reactions only: Insufficient data
- Topics/themes that resonated: Developer tools, practical AI comparisons, specific tool names
- What to avoid repeating: Abstract strategy or management framing
- Implication for THIS post: Lean into the contrast framing (overwhelm vs. clear path) and keep language practical/builder-oriented; Bold claim-first may outperform story-first based on early signals

## Angle Assignments

**Angle A: Story-first** — open with a personal moment of being overwhelmed by Gen AI content, let the roadmap emerge as the thing Altaf built to solve it for himself first.

**Angle B: Bold claim-first** — lead with the provocative framing: most Gen AI learning content wastes your time because it covers things that shift every week. Here's what actually stays stable.

**Angle C: Tactical/how-to-first** ← chosen adaptively

Rationale: Chose Tactical/how-to-first because **Decision Rule 2 matched** — the idea is literally a curated roadmap/checklist (a repeatable process for getting started with Gen AI). It teaches a concrete sequence of learning: start with fundamentals, validate where you are, fill the gap, then move to advanced. A tactical angle can lead with the practical outcome ("Here's the shortest path from zero to Gen AI-ready") and walk through the structure of the roadmap — something neither the story angle nor the bold claim angle will do structurally.

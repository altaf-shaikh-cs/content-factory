A few months ago I kept opening new tabs.

A paper on RAG. A tutorial on fine-tuning. A thread about agents. A video on embeddings.

Every week, something new was "the thing you need to know."

I wasn't learning Gen AI. I was just consuming it.

The problem wasn't discipline. It was having no map.

So I sat down and asked myself: if someone came to me with zero Gen AI knowledge, what would I actually tell them to learn first?

Not the stuff that changes every week. The stuff that stays.

I built a Level 1 roadmap. Small on purpose. No padding, no "you should also know about…" rabbit holes.

Just the core — the fundamentals that make everything else land when you get to it.

It's not exhaustive. That's the point.

If you're getting into Gen AI and feel like you're always behind, this might help you find where you actually are — and what to do next.

Check where you are on this: https://roadmap.sh/r/getting-started-with-gen-ai-level-1

Feedback welcome — I'll build the advanced version next.

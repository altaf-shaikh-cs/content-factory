## Final Post

Your company didn't adopt AI. It bought subscriptions.

There's a difference, and it has a name. Four names, actually.

Everyone I know "uses Claude". Almost nobody has configured Skills, Plugins, Projects, or Artifacts. That's the whole gap between dabbling and building it into how you work.

Skills: your standards, written once, applied automatically in every conversation. This is why one person's Claude output looks like their team's house style and yours looks like a generic chatbot.

Plugins: entire workflows packaged and installable. The new teammate installs one and the AI already knows how the team runs, before orientation ends.

Projects: hard walls between contexts. Client A's work never bleeds into client B's chat. If you handle anything confidential, this is not optional.

Artifacts: real files. Decks, documents, working tools. If your AI's output still lives and dies inside a chat window, you're using a fraction of it.

The uncomfortable version: a chatbot subscription makes people feel modern. Configuring these four makes people productive. Most teams stopped at feeling modern.

One line each, if you want the summary: Skills are consistency. Plugins are capability. Projects are separation. Artifacts are output.

Start with one Skill for one workflow that eats time every week. That's it. That's the adoption plan.

Which of the four are you actually using?

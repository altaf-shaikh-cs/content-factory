## Core Insight
In the AI era, developer fundamentals matter more, not less — AI is a multiplier, so strong foundations become 10x faster while weak foundations become 10x confused.

## Target Audience
Entry-to-mid career software engineers (primarily in Indian tech hubs) who are actively exploring AI tools and may be chasing frameworks/prompts at the expense of foundational thinking skills.

## Structure
1. The shift: coding is no longer the hard part
2. The real hard parts: knowing what to build, breaking down problems, debugging AI mistakes, system design, asking right questions
3. The tool-collector trap: most developers spending energy on collecting tools while weakening foundations
4. The amplification principle: AI multiplies what you already bring
5. The bifurcation: 10x faster (strong foundations) vs 10x confused (weak foundations)
6. The principle: don't chase tools, build the foundation
7. The permanence: tools and models change; the ability to think clearly compounds for decades

## CTA
Are you using AI at your work every day? How has your working style changed in the last 1-2 years?

## Tone Range
Conversational with authoritative backbone — like Altaf talking directly to a junior developer, not lecturing from above.

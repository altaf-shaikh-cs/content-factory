Every week I get messages that go something like this:

"Should I learn DSA or skip straight to AI tools?"
"Should I study system design or just master prompting?"
"Should I learn React or focus on AI agents?"

And every time, my answer is the same.

You're asking the wrong question.

Because here's what I keep seeing, talking to developers at all levels.

The ones genuinely thriving with AI are not the ones with the most tools.

They're the ones who had strong fundamentals before AI arrived.

Coding is no longer the hard part.

The hard part is:
- Knowing what to build and why
- Breaking down a problem before you write a single line
- Debugging when AI gives you a confident but wrong answer
- Designing systems that actually scale
- Asking a question that gets a useful answer

AI doesn't close the gap between strong and weak fundamentals.

It widens it.

A developer with strong foundations plus AI gets 10x faster.

A developer with weak foundations plus AI gets 10x more confused.

So every time someone asks me which thing to learn next, I give the same answer.

Don't chase the next tool.

Don't spend hours comparing frameworks.

Build the thinking skills that let you use any tool well.

Tools will change.
Frameworks will change.
AI models will change.

The ability to think clearly and solve real problems won't.

That skill compounds for decades.

Are you using AI at your work every day? How has your working style changed in the last 1-2 years?

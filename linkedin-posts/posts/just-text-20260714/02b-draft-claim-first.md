The developer who ignores AI is not the one falling behind.

The one falling behind is the developer who adopted every AI tool without fixing their fundamentals first.

Because AI doesn't balance you out.

It amplifies you.

Strong foundation plus AI: 10x faster.
Weak foundation plus AI: 10x more confused.

Coding is no longer the hard part of being a developer.

The hard parts are:
- Knowing what to build in the first place
- Breaking down complex problems cleanly
- Debugging when AI gives you confidently wrong answers
- Designing systems that don't fall apart at scale
- Asking the right question before reaching for any tool

These skills don't come from collecting more courses or prompts.

They come from building your foundation.

And yet most of the developers I talk to are doing the opposite.

Spending hours on the newest framework, the latest prompting technique, the hottest AI release.

While their fundamentals get weaker, not stronger.

So when someone asks me:

"Should I learn DSA?"
"Should I study system design?"
"Should I learn AI agents?"

My answer is still the same.

Build the foundation first.

Tools will change. Models will change. Frameworks will change.

The ability to think clearly and solve problems is the one skill that compounds.

Are you using AI at your work every day? How has your working style changed in the last 1-2 years?

I went through 25 lessons someone learned shipping features with Claude Code for months.

One line explained every bad AI coding day I've ever had:

"AI amplifies clarity or confusion. Your choice."

Every time my output was garbage, I traced it back. The spec lived in my head, half-formed. The chat was juggling three features. The context was whatever I felt like pasting.

The AI wasn't confused. I was. It just amplified me.

The lessons that stuck, from the list and from my own scars:

Write the spec before opening Claude. Planning is 80 percent of the outcome.

One feature per chat. The original phrasing is perfect: mixing features is coding drunk.

Commit after every working feature. Reverting beats fixing.

Never trust "should work". Loop the tests until they actually pass.

After every completion, ask it to review its own work and list what might be broken.

For fixes: "fix this without changing anything else." Cascade failures die there.

And keep a DONT_DO.md of past failures. The AI forgets. You shouldn't.

None of these are prompting tricks. They're just engineering discipline, pointed at a tool that multiplies whatever you bring to it.

If you're confused, the AI is too. Clarify for yourself first.

## Final Post

The best Claude Code tips have nothing to do with prompting.

A developer published 25 lessons from months of shipping features with it. Strip away the surface and one theme is doing all the work:

AI amplifies clarity or confusion. Your choice.

The tips that prove it:

Planning is 80 percent of success. Write the feature spec BEFORE opening Claude.

One feature per chat, always. Mixing features is coding drunk.

Git commit after every working feature. Reverting beats fixing.

"Should work" means it doesn't. Loop tests until they actually pass.

After every completion: "Review your work and list what might be broken."

For fixes: "Fix this without changing anything else."

Keep rules files under 100 lines. Concise beats comprehensive.

Keep a DONT_DO.md of past failures. AI forgets. You shouldn't.

Notice what's missing: no magic phrases, no secret system prompts.

It's the same discipline that made people good engineers before AI: clear specs, small scopes, honest verification, version control.

The tool didn't change the fundamentals. It raised the price of skipping them.

If you're confused, the AI is too. Clarify for yourself first.

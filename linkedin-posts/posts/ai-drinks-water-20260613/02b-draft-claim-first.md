Your AI prompt is not free.

Not in energy. Not in compute. And not in water.

Every AI query runs on physical servers. Servers generate heat. That heat has to go somewhere — and the answer is water. Fresh, drinkable water, fed into cooling systems so the infrastructure stays online.

While you're typing, a data center somewhere is drawing from a water supply.

And drinkable water is not infinite.

The conversation around AI is almost always about access, capability, and cost per token. Rarely about the physical supply chain keeping those tokens flowing.

You're not being asked to stop prompting. But calling AI usage "free" is only accurate from inside your billing dashboard.

The bill exists. It just shows up somewhere else — in resources most people never think about until they're scarce.

Maybe that's worth one more second of thought before the next query.

Do you think about what a single AI query actually costs — beyond the API bill?

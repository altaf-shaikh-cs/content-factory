## Final Post

The AI industry's most successful product is not a model.

It's the idea that AI has no cost.

Every tool launches with a generous free tier. Every benchmark celebrates cost-per-token dropping. Every pitch deck shows the intelligence layer getting cheaper.

And it is — for the person typing the prompt.

The data center cooling those servers doesn't run on efficiency headlines. It runs on electricity. And it runs on water. Fresh water. The kind that isn't free anywhere a drought has taken hold.

You're not being charged for it. But someone, somewhere, is absorbing it.

The democratization of AI narrative is true in one direction: access is easier. It's incomplete in another: the physical cost didn't disappear — it was just moved off your invoice and onto a ledger you never see.

That's not an argument against AI. It's an argument against pretending the cost doesn't exist.

Next time you hit send on a prompt — ask yourself if it was worth asking.

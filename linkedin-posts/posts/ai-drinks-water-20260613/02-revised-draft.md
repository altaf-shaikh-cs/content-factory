I typed a question. Hit send. Got an answer.

Two seconds. No charge. Barely a thought.

Except — somewhere between my question and that answer, a data center ran at full load.

Those servers generate heat. Heat needs cooling. Cooling takes water.

Fresh water. Drinkable water.

The kind that is becoming scarcer across the world while AI usage compounds every quarter.

I didn't pour a single glass. I didn't sweat. I didn't even stand up.

And yet something moved in the background that no invoice will ever itemize.

The bill exists. It just shows up somewhere else — in resources most people never think about until they're scarce.

Every "just a quick summary." Every "can you rewrite this." Every casual prompt adds to a tab that exists nowhere in your dashboard.

The intelligence layer feels weightless. The infrastructure behind it isn't.

You don't need to stop prompting. But maybe it's worth knowing what's actually behind the Send button.

Do you think about what a single AI query actually costs — beyond the API bill?

## Core Insight
The difference between AI workflows that need you and AI infrastructure that doesn't is a single architectural decision: does it run on its own, or do you have to call it?

## Target Audience
Builders, indie hackers, and developers who are using AI tools today (call-and-response style) and thinking about how to go further — autonomous, scheduled, infrastructure-level.

## Structure
SINGLE — one beat: name the contrast (capability vs infrastructure), place yourself in the story (/loop changed that for me), invite the reader to imagine their own first loop.

## CTA / Reply hook
A reply-inviting question that is concrete and easy to answer: "What's the first workflow you'd convert?" No links in this post — the idea stands alone.

## Tone
Punchy + contrarian lean, grounded in a personal moment. Confident without being preachy.

## Hashtags
None — this post lives or dies by the hook quality, not tag discovery.

## Image Brief
Two qualitative contrast elements:
- "capability" (manual trigger, one-off, waits for you)
- "infrastructure" (autonomous, scheduled, keeps running)
Tagline: "Set it. Let it loop."
The image should carry the core contrast cleanly — bold typography, no clutter. Landscape 1600×900 for in-stream, Square 1080×1080 for quote-card variant.

## Angle Assignments

- **Angle 1 — Viral / reach play:** Open on the quotable contrast itself — "A tool you call is a capability. A process that runs on its own is infrastructure." — then place it in context (/loop changed that for me) and close on the question. Engineered for bookmarks and screenshot-shares; this line is the payoff.

- **Angle 2 — Build-in-public / story play:** Open on the concrete moment — "Just set up my first real AI loop." — then describe what it actually does (the folder-drop pipeline), close on the honest day-one admission ("Still feels weird to watch") and the question. Targets replies via relatability and curiosity.

- **Angle 3 — Contrarian / debate play:** Open on the accusation — calling AI workflows "infrastructure" when you still trigger them manually is marketing, not engineering — then draw the line (infrastructure runs without you, most AI workflows don't, a loop does), close on a reply-inviting challenge. Targets quote-tweets and disagreement-as-engagement.

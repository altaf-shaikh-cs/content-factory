Six screens in. UI looked clean.

Nothing worked.

That was my first real lesson building an app with AI from scratch.

---

2/4

I gave it design references. Generated the code. Fixed every bug.

It produced output fast. Every single time.

"The app works" and "the app is useful" turned out to be completely different problems.

---

3/4

I ended up refining it one screen at a time. One flow at a time.

The agent could design components in isolation.
It couldn't build something a user picks up and understands in 10 seconds.

That part is still yours.

---

4/4

AI accelerates the build. It doesn't replace thinking about how the build needs to feel.

You still hold the vision. You still test the whole flow.

Building something non-trivial with AI right now — where did your first real wall hit?

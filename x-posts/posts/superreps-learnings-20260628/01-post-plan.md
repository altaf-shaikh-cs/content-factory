## Format Decision
THREAD — 4 tweets. The idea has a clear before→during→lesson→CTA arc that loses its punch when compressed to a single tweet. The setup (AI felt easy), the wall (six screens, nothing worked), the hard truth (AI stitches components, not flows), and the builder question are four genuinely distinct beats — not padding, not expansion for its own sake. Tweet 1 must stand alone as a scroll-stopper; tweets 2–4 pay it off.

## Core Insight
AI accelerates building components; it cannot own the product thinking that turns components into a working, useful flow — that part still belongs to you.

## Target Audience
Builders, developers, and indie hackers who are using AI coding tools to ship apps — specifically those who've hit the point where the AI "works" but the product doesn't.

## Structure
1. Hook tweet — the contrast that stops the scroll ("AI is fast at producing output. Usefulness takes longer.")
2. The moment — "Six screens in. UI looked clean. Nothing worked." Concrete, specific, relatable.
3. The lesson — screen-by-screen refinement; AI designs components in isolation, can't stitch a flow a user picks up and understands. You still hold the vision.
4. CTA — invite replies with a builder question about where their first real wall appeared.

## CTA / Reply hook
End with a direct, easy-to-answer question: "Building something non-trivial with AI right now — where did your first real wall hit?" No link needed; this idea doesn't require one.

## Tone
Build-in-public / honest. Punchy and specific — not self-deprecating, not triumphant. Just what happened and what it taught.

## Hashtags
None. The builder community on X finds this content through the hook + engagement, not tags.

## Image Brief
Key contrast pair: "Looks like an app" vs "Works like an app"
Stats/moments:
  - 6 screens built before anything worked
  - Screen-by-screen refinement (not a single prompt)
  - "AI designs components. You design flows."
Tagline: "AI builds fast. Useful takes time."

## Angle Assignments

- **Angle 1 — Viral / reach play:** Lead with the sharpest contrast line in the idea as a standalone claim. Make it quotable enough to screenshot. "AI is fast at producing output. It is slow at producing usefulness." — strip the story, just the theorem. Then one concrete proof ("Built 6 screens. None of them worked.") and a hard close. Reposts come from the line, not the narrative.

- **Angle 2 — Story / build-in-public play:** Open on the concrete moment ("Six screens in. UI looked clean. Nothing worked."), then move through what the experience actually taught — the screen-by-screen grind, the realization, the takeaway. First-person and specific. Ends on an open question that invites replies ("Where did your first real wall hit?").

- **Angle 3 — Contrarian / debate play:** Push against "just vibe-code it" / "one prompt builds an app" culture. Open on the claim that contradicts the hype, back it with the concrete experience, close with the real model of how it actually works. Invites disagreement and quote-tweets from people who think the tools are further along than they are.
